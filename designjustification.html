DESIGN JUSTIFICATION

DESIGN ELEMENTS

One of the most dominant design elements we focussed on with creating and designing the website was contrast. Our main colour palette (the pink and blue) are already heavily contrasting tones and so within the website we had to take this into consideration with making the text readable and to not distract the viewer from the natural flow of the website.

Through creating contrast between the colourful background we aimed to create emphasis on the important texts and key elements of our brand. Emphasis, as a design principle, deals with the parts of a design that are meant to stand out and for us it was incredibly important to clearly highlight for viewers that there are two different consumer segments we are targeting. This is why we made a clear distinction between participants and “distributors” on the home page.

We also heavily relied on the design element proportion when designing our website. Our brand Mind Label has an extremely visual aesthetic which is why we used the design element proportion to lead the website’s viewers through a visual journey. We then minimally used the element repetition to keep our viewers engaged and surprised about what they will come across on the different pages of our website. This conceptually matches with our brand’s message, which is about moving your mind, opening up your perspective. With this we do not imply that the website lacks consistency as we stuck strictly to our fonts, colours and basic organization (e.g. all Archivo Black headers are white).

CHOICE OF FONTS

The Google Fonts we used on our website are Archivo Black and quicksand. As mentioned before, this is also a great example of the strong contrast we wanted to create to represent our creativity and diversity. Archivo Black is a font which is very powerful: bold, fixed and forthcoming. Quicksand on the other hand, represents more calm, cute and airy. These two fonts reinforce each other again by the difference. It was important that they both come across as powerful for the content. In this way we could indicate even more clearly the difference regarding titles and paragraphs.

By using only these two fonts, we achieved a consistently looking website. We used sans serif fonts because we learned last year that this best for readability for web and digital content. (Source: Laura Pardo)

We used different sizes and styles within these font families. This creates Hierarchy according to the Design Principles.
(Source: Design Principles Slides IMEM Year 1 )

STYLE GUIDE


We developed our style guide during our assignment for Brand Developmet (Road Map.) We concluded very soon this would be an outstanding style. We picked some bright and overwhelming colours to express the creativity of the brand. We didn’t want to go with a dull pallet since part of our vision concluded fighting close-mindedness.

USE OF COLOURS

These highly contrasting colours also represent the strong diversity we want to share throughout the world. These colours gave us the opportunity to highlight and separate content intensely.

The muted red on the website is energetic without being aggressive. Blue is also used extensively to represent responsibility. Dark blues are more strong and reliable. Blue is also associated with peace and has spiritual and religious connotations in many cultures and traditions (for example, the Virgin Mary is generally depicted wearing blue robes).Purple is a combination of red and blue and takes on some attributes of both. It’s associated with creativity and imagination, too. Light purples are softer and are associated with spring and romance. Click here for the source. Every Background contains this same gradient. According to the Design Principles this creates Unity. This is part of the site’s identity.
